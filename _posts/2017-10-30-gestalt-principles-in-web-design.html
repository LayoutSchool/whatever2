There is an in-built system that allows us to make sense of the world. These principles are: closure, common fate, figure-ground, good continuation, proximity, similarity, symmetry, and uniform connectedness.

Closure

The principle of closure describes situations in which people, given only a part of the picture, will fill in the blanks.

Another way to describe closure is that people tend to look at a complex grouping of visual elements and try to form a single image from it.

Common Fate

The principle of common fate is the mechanism by which we perceive things that are moving together to be related.

Figure-ground

The principle of figure-ground is the way in which figures in the foreground are distinguishable from figures in the background.

A practical example is this McDonald's ad which turns pedestrian crossing stripes into french fries.
